The HMN Solution

Emerging Antagonistic Forces

The current landscape faces increasing threats to autonomy, privacy, and transparency:

  • Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs): Introduce mechanisms for unprecedented control over individual financial autonomy.
  • Social Credit Systems: Enable mass surveillance that imposes behavioral monitoring on individuals.
  • Data Breaches: Frequent data and privacy breaches within tech corporations erode trust and compromise personal information.
  • Centralized AI: Controlled by a few entities, centralized AI facilitates mass surveillance and information control, concentrating power among a few corporations, who then hold the majority of resources, decision-making authority, and capital.

Privacy

Legacy governance systems and organizations expose user data to breaches. The HMN incorporates zk-SNARKs to secure privacy in governance and voting, enabling verifiable yet secure participation in the network.

Reshaping the DAO

Existing DAOs struggle with collusion, whale manipulation, low participation, and inefficient voting. The HMN employs quadratic voting, allowing participants to express their preference intensity proportional to the collective decisions in the network, reducing whale manipulation. Liquid democracy is adopted where participants can delegate votes to select representatives. Modular governance structure supports scalable, high-participation models that adapt dynamically as the network states evolve.

Consensus

HMN’s consensus algorithms leverage distributed inputs from human and AI agents to build a highly efficient governance system. This system dynamically aligns human and machine directives, helping reach consensus at scale and coordinating collective decisions across the network.

The Infrastructure for Network States

These tech stacks provide the necessary infrastructure for an efficient, privacy-preserving, and democratic governance system that operates within both physical and cyberspace.
